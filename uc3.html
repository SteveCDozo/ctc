Decreasing Risk of Data Breaches through Crowdsourced Clients

TARGETED ISSUE

All companies are at risk

Many different kinds of attacks have been revealed recently, which involved new sophisticated ways of stealing data from companies. In 2017, credit reporting agency Equifax hired cybersecurity firm Mandiant to conduct a forensic analysis, which revealed a massive data breach affecting 145.5 million U.S. consumers. A popular ride hailing service, Uber, failed to disclose a data breach last year for more than 57 million Uber drivers and riders. Although the biggest, headline-grabbing cyber attacks tend to only hit the biggest companies, all companies actually need to be aware of the risk of data breaches.
SOLUTION PURPOSE

Work together with us, against them

Many businesses have taken steps to protect their data, but the average size of the data breaches in 2017 still increased by 1.8 percent to more than 24,000 records. Sometimes companies can hardly respond quickly enough to the issue to avoid any losses. Our solutions connects as many companies as possible to enable them to respond together to the latest cyber attack issue before being hacked and share the solution with each other at the same time.
CORE TECHNOLOGY

Stop hackers in their tracks

A honeypot is a computer security mechanism set to detect, deflect, or, in some manner, counteract attempts at unauthorized use of information systems. It consists of fake data that appears to be a part of the site, but is actually isolated and monitored. Therefore, once a company’s honeypot is attacked, our expert team can actually get data on the attack.

SOLUTION PROCESS

How does it all work?

  • 1
    Hacker Triggers The Honeypot

    The solution team sets a honeypot into each client's system.
    When a client is attacked, the hacker actually reaches the honeypot.

  • 2
    The System Responds

    The system immediately extracts the log information from the client's firewall and sends it to our expert team.

  • 3
    Experts Analyze the Log

    The expert team reviews the log and sends it out to the network after ensuring it does not contain any confidential information. They also analyze the log to determine how the attack occurred and work on a solution. If a solution is found, it is sent to the network.

  • 4
    Clients Analyze the Log

    The network of clients receives the log and each client also tries finding a solution since it will help them improve their own systems. If a solution is found, it is sent out to other clients in the network.
