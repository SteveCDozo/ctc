Task Recruitment through Crowdsourced Employees

TARGETED ISSUE

Finding employees to work on new tasks can be tough

Small and medium sized businesses without an experienced workforce may find themselves requesting help or outsourcing work to bigger companies with more knowledge in the field. Managers of big companies often have trouble finding an employee to work on these new client requests or tasks. With over 1,000 employees at a big company and numerous requests to be taken care of, it may take quite some time to send out emails and wait for responses.
SOLUTION PURPOSE

Find unused talent in your corporation

Don't just rely on your employees to be able to identify BEC scams when they're continously evolving everyday. Our scam identification network harnesses the power of different companies around the world. Scam email data is collected from participating companies to train identification models using machine learning and data science. We enable businesses to identify scams systematically and effectively.
SOLUTION PROCESS

What does the process look like?

  • 1
    Install Email Plugin

    Every employee of the participating company installs a plugin on his work computer

  • 2
    Report Scams

    Every time an employee receives an email, he/she will be given an option to report it as a scam email or label it as a trusted email

  • 3
    Local Analysis

    The local server will store and analyze the local data
    It will share the scam email data with the remote server if the company chooses to have this service

  • 4
    Collect All Data

    The remote server will aggregate data from all participating companies and build stronger models

  • 5
    Assess Risk

    With these models, the plugin will assess the risk for each email received and inform the user

ADDITIONAL FEATURES

Extra precautions to keep your business safe

Apart from the big data driven process, the network can also identify compromised emails by using the open standard DMARC and keeping records of email sending activities. DMARC can help identify spoofing attacks which fake their email addresses via corrupted server. DMARC attaches a signature to each email sent from a legitimate server.

Scenario

Description
(1) Spoofing Attack The user receives an email which does not have a DMARC signature. Our system simply blocks this email.
(2) Display Name Deception The email is sent from a legitimate server, but it fakes its display name as a trusted insider. Our system finds the name on the list of trusted contacts but the address does not match, so it alerts the recipient or just blocks this email.
(3) Comprimised Account The sender is verified as another user of the network, but the system can’t find the sent record of the email, so it will alert the user that it is potentially compromised.
(4) Safe Email The email is sent by another user of the network and the activity is recorded by the system, so it’s fine.
